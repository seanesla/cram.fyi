# Demo Study Guide

Disclaimer: This is a small demo study guide for the public repo. It is meant to show the structure of the app and should be replaced with your own private course material when you use the app for real studying.

Study Workflow

Understand how a flashcard deck is reviewed from top to bottom, or in a shuffled order.
Recognize what the mastery labels mean and how they change as you review cards.
Describe how queueing lets you keep typing while the assistant is still answering.

Cards and Content

Understand how a flashcard prompt should be short, focused, and tied to one idea.
Recognize why the assistant should answer in plain language for a beginner.
Describe how a card can be rewritten in a new wording without changing the core idea.

Chat Assistant

Understand the model selector and reasoning effort selector.
Recognize when to use a fresh chat instead of continuing an older one.
Describe how slash commands and the command menu help you switch modes quickly.

Progress Tracking

Understand the context meter and why it shows approximate token usage.
Recognize why compaction helps when a thread gets too long.
Describe how the queue count shows messages waiting to be sent.

Sharing and Deployment

Understand why demo content should stay separate from private material.
Recognize the difference between a public app shell and a private study deck.
Describe why a clean repo should not include any sensitive files or course content.
